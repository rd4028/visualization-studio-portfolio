# Microplastics In Australian Coastlines

## Overview

This was a group data visualisation project for 41193 IS Visualization Studio. The project investigated the presence of microplastics in Australian coastal environments and how contamination can affect marine animals, seafood safety, and Australians who consume local seafood.

My individual section focused on the Australian Government as the stakeholder. I built Tableau visualisations to help answer where intervention should be prioritised and which polymers or health effects should receive stronger regulatory attention.

## Project Question

How does the presence of microplastics in the Australian coastal environment affect the health and survival of Australian marine animals, and in turn, how does this affect Australians who consume local seafood?

## My Individual Stakeholder Focus

Stakeholder: Australian Government

My individual analysis addressed two questions:

1. Which regions and fisheries should the government prioritise for intervention?
2. Which polymers and health impacts should be prioritised in regulations?

## Visualisations Created

| Visualisation | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Base map | Identify coastal contamination hotspots and intervention zones |
| Tree map | Compare fisheries and polymer types by contamination level |
| Highlight table | Compare species exposure across Australian regions |
| Scatter plot | Prioritise polymers by prevalence and health impact |
| Stacked bar chart | Compare polymer presence in edible and non-edible seafood tissues |
| Bubble chart | Link organisms, body systems, and polymer-related health effects |
