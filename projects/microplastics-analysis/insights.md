# Insights And Recommendations

## Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Some coastal zones showed stronger contamination signals | Regional hotspot map highlighted areas including Northern Adelaide and parts of the Northern Territory coastline | Helps government prioritise monitoring, resource allocation, and clean-up planning |
| Certain fisheries and polymer types appeared more affected | Tree map compared contamination by fishery and polymer type | Supports targeted clean-up efforts and stronger controls for high-risk polymer sources |
| Species exposure varied by region | Highlight table showed fish exposure in NT and QLD, and crustacean exposure in SA and WA | Helps design region-specific and species-specific intervention strategies |
| Some polymers require higher regulatory attention | Scatter plot used prevalence and health-impact severity to identify priority polymers, including PP and PE | Gives policymakers a clear framework for deciding which polymers need closer review |
| Edible seafood tissues still showed microplastic presence | Stacked bar chart compared edible and non-edible tissues | Connects environmental contamination to possible human exposure through seafood consumption |
| Health impacts were connected to organisms and body systems | Bubble chart linked polymers with organisms and body systems, with strong signals around immune and gastrointestinal effects | Helps prioritise health-risk communication and follow-up testing |

## Recommendations

- Prioritise monitoring in coastal hotspots where contamination signals are strongest.
- Focus intervention strategies on regions and fisheries with higher exposure patterns.
- Review polymers with both high prevalence and serious health-effect links.
- Strengthen seafood safety monitoring where edible tissue exposure is identified.
- Use visual dashboards to support government reporting, public communication, and evidence-based policy decisions.
- Continue improving data quality, especially consistent region, polymer, species, and tissue classification.

## Reflection

This project strengthened my ability to translate environmental data into stakeholder-focused evidence. The most useful learning was connecting data preparation, visual design, and policy-style recommendations into one clear analytical story.
